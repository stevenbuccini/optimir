Business Associate

What They Do

Optimir is looking to hire Associates for the fall semester. Associates will go through a semester long training period. The first half of the semester will be spent learning essential consulting skills, while the second half will involve actual project work in which Associates will be staffed onto Optimir projects.

Associates can expect a fast paced and rigorous training period. Associates will learn the how to use tools such as PowerPoint and Excel. They will also be instructed in the fundamentals behind competitive strategy, business growth, and market research. When staffed on projects, Associates can expect to populate models, analyze data collected from primary and secondary research, and provide insight into business decisions for clients.



Responsibilities

- Must be able to attend a two-hour Sunday meeting and a one hour Thursday meeting.
- Must be able to commit four hours out of meeting time to Optimir.
- Will be given biweekly assignments during training process.
- Will be tasked with data collection, data analysis, and slide creation during client project work
- Must maintain integrity and professionalism during course of training process and client project work



Qualifications

- Must be currently enrolled as an undergraduate student at UC Berkeley for the Fall semester
- 3.5 GPA or higher strongly preferred (does not apply for entering freshmen)
- Critical thinking and analytical abilities
- Strong interpersonal and communication skills

Technology Associate

What They Do

Optimir is looking to hire Technology Associates for the Fall semester. Technology Associates combine business strategy with technical expertise to provide software advisory services to our tech clients. In short, they are hybrids of software engineers and business consultants. Technology Associates will go through the same business training as our associate interns in order to obtain an understanding of how to best integrate their software skills into a business strategy setting. Technology associates can expect to assist tech clients by working on back front end code for their products and services, or designing UI material for websites. They can also expect to provide advisory knowledge to Optimir Associates and Executive team members.

An individual Technology Associate will focus on either software engineering or graphic/UI design work. Please apply to the focus that you feel is most applicable to you based off of your prior experience and personal interests. This job posting is for Technology Associates with a Software Engineering Focus.



Responsibilities

- Must attend a one hour Sunday meeting and one hour Wednesday meeting each week
- Must be able to commit two hours of out of meeting time to Optimir
- Will be given bi-weekly assignments during training process
- Will be tasked with satisfying any client back end programming needs during client project work
- Must maintain integrity and professionalism during course of training process and client project work



Qualifications

- Currently enrolled as an undergraduate student at UC Berkeley for the Fall semester
- Knowledge of a programming language such as Python, Ruby/Rails, Java, etc.
- Knowledge of HTML, CSS, and Javascript preferred
- 3.0 GPA or higher strongly preferred (does not apply for entering freshmen)
- Critical thinking and analytical abilities
- Strong interpersonal and communication skills
